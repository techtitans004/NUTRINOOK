Tomato Basil Penne

Ingredients

  • 12 oz (340g) penne pasta
  • 1 tablespoon salt (for boiling water)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cups ripe tomatoes, chopped (or 2 cans of diced tomatoes)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for a bit of heat)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar (optional, for added depth of flavor)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra grated Parmesan cheese

Nutritional Information

  • Calories: 350 kcal
  • Total Fat: 10g
  • Saturated Fat: 2g
  • Cholesterol: 5mg (if Parmesan cheese is used)
  • Sodium: 200mg
  • Total Carbohydrates: 55g
  • Dietary Fiber: 5g
  • Sugars: 8g
  • Protein: 10g

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Add the penne pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente.
  3. Drain the pasta and set aside.
  4. While the pasta is cooking, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  5. Add the minced garlic and chopped onion, and saut√© until the onion is translucent and the garlic is fragrant, about 3-4 minutes.
  6. Add the chopped tomatoes (or canned diced tomatoes) to the skillet. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the tomatoes start to break down and form a sauce, about 10-15 minutes.
  7. Season with red pepper flakes (if using), salt, and pepper to taste.
  8. Stir in the chopped fresh basil and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  9. If using, add the balsamic vinegar for extra flavor depth.
  10. Add the cooked penne pasta to the skillet with the tomato basil sauce.
  11. Toss to coat the pasta evenly with the sauce.
  12. If using, sprinkle in the grated Parmesan cheese and toss again until melted and combined.
  13. Serve the tomato basil penne hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves and extra grated Parmesan cheese if desired
